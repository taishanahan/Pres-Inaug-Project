The Presidents

1897-1901: William McKinley

  1. President McKinley was born and raised in Niles, Ohio. He served 14 years in the House of Representatives and 8 years as the Governor of Ohio before becoming president. During his presidency he led the nation to victory in the Spanish-American war and raised protective tariffs to promote American industry.

1901-1909: Theodore Roosevelt

  1. President Roosevelt was born and raised in New York City. He served in the American army before moving into politics. During his presidency he directed America towards more progressive reforms and stronger foreign policies. He was the first president to receive the Noble Peace Prize for his work in mediating the Russo-Japanese War.

1909-1913: William H. Taft

1913-1921: Woodrow Wilson

  1. President Wilson was born and raised in Staunton, Virginia. Prior to politics, he practiced law. In 1910, he was elected as the Governor of New Jersey. During his presidency he led the Progressive Movement and led America into World War I pledging to make the world a safe place for democracy.

1921-1923: Warren G. Harding

  1. President Harding was born and raised in Blooming Grove, Ohio. Prior to politics, he worked as an editor for many newspapers. During his presidency he led the country out of postwar depression and embraced the advancements of technology. His presidency was also tarnished by scandals.

1923-1929: Calvin Coolidge

  1. President Coolidge was born and raised in Plymouth, Vermont. He pursued law for a short time before transitioning into politics. He quickly grew through the ranks and became the Governor of Massachusetts. During his presidency he strengthened the American economy and shrank the regulatory rule of the federal government.

1929-1933: Herbert Hoover

  1. President Hoover was born in West Branch, Iowa but grew up in Oregon. Prior to politics, he worked as a mining engineer and briefly worked in China for a private corporation as Chinaâ€™s lead engineer. During his presidency he continued the economic policies of President Coolidge and fed war-torn Europe by providing agricultural products from the United States.

1933-1945: Franklin D. Roosevelt

1945-1953: Harry S. Truman

  1. President Truman was born and raised in Lamar, Missouri. He served in the American Army during World War I. After his stint in the army, he transitioned to politics and was elected as a Senator. During his presidency he ordered the drop of atomic bombs over Japanese cities and promoted the preservation of peace globally. He personally oversaw the signing of the charter of the United Nations hoping it would bring peace and prosperity worldwide.

1953-1961: Dwight D. Eisenhower

  1. President Eisenhower was born in Texas but was raised in Abilene, Kansas. He excelled in the American army and oversaw the troops that invaded France during World War II. Soon after he became Supreme Commander of the NATO forces. He became president in 1953 without any real prior political experience. During his presidency he strengthened the American army and eased the Cold War tensions.

1961-1963: John F. Kennedy

1963-1969: Lyndon B. Johnson

1969-1974: Richard M. Nixon

1974-1977: Gerald R. Ford

  1. President Gerald Ford was born and raised in Omaha, Nebraska. After former President Nixon resigned due to the infamous Watergate Scandal, President Ford took office, as he quickly pardoned Nixon, an unpopular opinion. With tensions high from the Cold War, President Ford limits underground nuclear testing for the Soviet Union with a treaty. Back at home, he cut inflation and created nearly four million jobs, boosting the overall economy.

1977-1981: Jimmy Carter

1981-1989: Ronald Reagan

1989-1993: George H.W. Bush

1993-2001: Bill Clinton

2001-2009: George W. Bush

2009-2017: Barack Obama

  1. President Barrack Obama was born and raised in Honolulu, Hawaii until eventually attending Columbia University. He was inaugurated as the first African American to hold the prestigious office. Through Obamacare, he regulated the U.S. healthcare system brining affordability and access to healthcare toe many Americans. Also, through efforts on the War on Terror, President Obama announces the death of Osama Bin Laden after an overseas operation, who was responsible for the September 11, 2001, terrorist attacks. Through his support of LGBTQ, he announces the legal support of same-sex marriage.

2017-2021: Donald J. Trump

2021-2025: Joseph R. Biden

  1. Current President Joe Biden was born and raised in Scranton, Pennsylvania in 1942. Coming into a pandemic environment after COVID-19, President Biden rolled out trillions of dollars in healthcare funding and stimulus payments to boost the economy, which inevitably fell into a depression because of non-essential businesses being forced to close. With the aid of popular COVID-19 vaccines from Pfizer and Moderna, life turned around in the United States, only as tensions continued to rise within the War between Russia and Ukraine.
